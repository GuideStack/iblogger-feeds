# Template: 30/60/90 Day Plan (New QA Lead)

> **How to use:** Fill this out in your first week and review it with your manager. It turns the [First 90 Days procedure](../01-first-90-days.md) into a concrete, shareable commitment. Keep it to one page. Update it at each 30-day mark. The goal isn't to predict everything — it's to align expectations and show deliberate progress.

# 30/60/90 Plan — Dara, QA Lead

**Start date:** 2026-06-01
**Manager:** Sophea (Eng Manager)
**Mandate:** "Reduce bugs escaping to production and make releases less stressful."

## 🎯 First 30 Days — LISTEN & ASSESS
| Goal | Measure |
|:-----|:--------|
| 1-on-1 with all 4 QA engineers | 4/4 done |
| Meet Dev leads, PM, Support, DevOps | 6 partners met |
| Shadow the June release | Done |
| QA assessment report | Shared with Sophea by day 28 |

## 🗺️ Days 31–60 — PLAN
| Goal | Measure |
|:-----|:--------|
| Draft test strategy | Reviewed by dev lead + PM |
| Top 3: flaky E2E, manual regression, slow triage | Roadmap agreed |
| Buy-in for automation time | 20% sprint capacity approved |

## 🚀 Days 61–90 — EXECUTE
| Goal | Measure |
|:-----|:--------|
| Quick win: stabilize flaky E2E | Flaky rate 18% → <5% |
| Quick win: standard bug template | Adopted by team |
| Cadence | Daily triage + weekly 1-on-1s live |
| Metrics | Escaped defects, cycle time, flaky rate dashboards |

## 🆘 What I need
- 20% team capacity for automation in Q3
- Intro to the payments domain owner
