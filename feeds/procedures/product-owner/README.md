# 🎯 Product Owner Playbook

A practical, step-by-step playbook for someone stepping into a **Product Owner role for the first time, in a new company/workspace.** (Agile/Scrum team role — *not* the Project Manager, and *not* the executive Business Owner/sponsor.) It covers both halves of the job:

1. **Onboarding** — what *you* do in your first 90 days to land well.
2. **Craft** — the vision, backlog, stories, prioritization, and stakeholder work you *produce* to maximize value.

> Built on one principle: **the PO owns the product backlog and is accountable for the value the team delivers.** You decide *what* and *why*; the team decides *how*; the Scrum Master owns the process; the PM (if present) owns delivery and timeline. Your power comes from making value clear and the next-most-valuable thing obvious — so spend your first month understanding what creates value here before you re-order anything.

Each procedure follows the house format: a **Mermaid swimlane diagram**, a portable **ASCII flow**, and a **step-by-step responsibility table** — so it works in GitHub, Confluence, or a terminal.

## 🧭 How to Use This Playbook

```
You just got the PO role
        │
        ▼
  Read 01 (First 90 Days) ── fill the 30/60/90 template
        │
        ▼
  Days 1–14:  LISTEN  → learn the customer + product, 1-on-1s
  Days 15–30: ASSESS  → 02 + score the 6 dimensions
  Days 31–60: PLAN    → 03 (Vision & Roadmap) + re-order the backlog (04, 05)
  Days 61–90: EXECUTE → 06 + run refinement→review, accept/reject, outcome metrics
```

> **New to the role and short on time?** Read **01** today, skim **02**, and start your 1-on-1s tomorrow. Do NOT re-rank the backlog this week — listen first.
