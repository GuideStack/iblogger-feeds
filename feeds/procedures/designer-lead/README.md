# 🎨 Designer Lead / Design Lead Playbook

A practical, step-by-step playbook for someone stepping into a **Designer Lead / Design Lead role for the first time, in a new company/workspace.** It covers both halves of the job:

1. **Onboarding** — what *you* do in your first 90 days to land well.
2. **Design leadership** — the design system, critique culture, research practice, and people growth you *build* for the team.

> Built on one principle: **lead through craft credibility and influence, not authority.** Design has no control over what ships — it earns its seat through trust, evidence, and visibly better outcomes. The hardest shift is from top individual contributor designer to **multiplier** — you still design, but your real output is now the team's quality, consistency, and growth. Spend your first month earning trust and understanding the product before you change it.

Each procedure follows the house format: a **Mermaid swimlane diagram**, a portable **ASCII flow**, and a **step-by-step responsibility table** — so it works in GitHub, Confluence, or a terminal.

## 📋 Ready-to-Use Templates

Each is a **blank template** + a **filled example** (same continuous story — a new Designer Lead named Lakena joining a product with an inconsistent UI, no design system, and design treated as a pixel-pushing service desk).

| Template | Use it for |
|:---------|:-----------|
| [30/60/90 Day Plan](./templates/30-60-90-plan-template.md) | Aligning expectations with your manager in week 1 |
| [Design Assessment](./templates/design-assessment-template.md) | The output of your Phase 2 audit (6 dimensions, 1–5 scores) |
| [Design Critique](./templates/design-critique-template.md) | A structured critique agenda — context, goals, rounds, decisions |
| [1-on-1 Notes](./templates/one-on-one-template.md) | Per-report running notes + first-meeting discovery questions |

## 🧭 How to Use This Playbook

```
You just got the Designer Lead role
        │
        ▼
  Read 01 (First 90 Days) ── fill the 30/60/90 template
        │
        ▼
  Days 1–14:  LISTEN  → 1-on-1 template, discovery questions
  Days 15–30: ASSESS  → 02 + Design Assessment template
  Days 31–60: PLAN    → 03 (Design System & Ops) + critique/research direction
  Days 61–90: EXECUTE → 04, 05, 06 + critique culture, research cadence, growth
```

> **New to the role and short on time?** Read **01** today, skim **02**, and start your 1-on-1s tomorrow. Keep designing so you stay credible — just not on the critical path. Everything else can wait until you understand the team.
