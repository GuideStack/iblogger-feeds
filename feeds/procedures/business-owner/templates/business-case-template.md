# Business Case — BillFlow Onboarding Revamp

**Author:** Dara Chea · **Decider:** Dara Chea (BO) · **Recommends:** Product Owner
**Funding ask:** 2 engineers + 1 designer for one quarter (~$180k loaded)

**Problem:** New BillFlow accounts activate at only 34% (reach first successful invoice within 14 days). The other 66% mostly churn before month 2 — this is the single biggest leak in net revenue retention (currently 96%). Fixing activation directly feeds the north-star (weekly active paying accounts).

**Options:**
- **A — Do nothing:** activation stays ~34%, NRR keeps leaking, growth stays flat. Baseline cost $0, but cost of inaction is the lost quarter.
- **B — Onboarding revamp (recommended):** guided setup + sample data + first-invoice checklist. ~$180k, 1 quarter.
- **C — Buy a 3rd-party onboarding tool:** ~$60k/yr + integration; faster but poor fit for billing-specific flows, and it's a non-differentiator we'd rather own here.

**Expected value:** lifting activation 34% → 50% on ~1,400 new accounts/qtr ≈ **$300–450k incremental ARR/yr** at current ARPA, payback ≈ 6–8 months. Gross-margin neutral.

**Key assumption:** activation is the binding constraint on retention (confidence: High — backed by cohort data and 6 customer interviews). If wrong, the churn-save bet (separate case) is the fallback.

**Recommendation:** Fund Option B for one quarter. Success metric: new-cohort activation ≥ 48% by end of quarter. Stage-gate review at 6 weeks — continue only if the first cohort shows ≥ +4pts.
