**Owner:** Dara Chea — Product line: "BillFlow" (B2B SaaS billing for SMBs)
**Mandate:** "Get BillFlow growing again — revenue's been flat for 5 quarters and nobody can say what our strategy is."

**30 days:** Met all 3 delivery leads (PM, PO, EM) — team is capable but rudderless, no clear north-star. Talked to 6 customers (4 retained, 2 churned). Built the baseline: ARR flat at ~$4.2M, gross margin 71%, net revenue retention 96% (leaking), CAC payback 16 months. Assessment shared with exec day 28 — headline: solid product, weak focus and no value metric to steer by.

**60 days:** Vision = "the easiest billing system for SMBs to switch *to*." North-star = **weekly active paying accounts**. Set 3 objectives (lift activation, cut churn, expand within accounts). Budget concentrated: 70% defend/grow core, 20% expansion, 10% one new bet — stopped funding a stalled "enterprise" side-quest.

**90 days:** Funded 2 bets — onboarding revamp (PO-owned) and a churn-save play (PM-owned). Published a RAPID decision-rights chart (PO owns backlog order; I own go/no-go and budget). KPI dashboard live with activation + trial→paid as leading indicators. Activation up 6pts in the first cohort.

**Need:** authority to redeploy the freed enterprise budget without a fresh board cycle; air cover with Sales, who keep injecting custom one-offs.
