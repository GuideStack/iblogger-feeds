# 30/60/90 Plan — Sothea, Support / Customer Success Lead

**Start date:** 2026-06-01
**Manager:** Priya (Head of CX)
**Mandate:** "Stop the bleeding — get response times under control and stop losing customers we never saw at risk."

**30 days:** 1-on-1 with all 5 agents + 2 CSMs; mapped Eng, Product, and Sales stakeholders; shadowed the queue for a week. Support & CS Assessment shared with Priya by day 28. Headline: **no SLAs exist; a 2-week backlog of ~180 tickets; CSAT untracked; churn is unmonitored and rising (~3 logos last quarter, all surprises); engineering ignores support's bug reports — the feedback loop is a black hole.** Scores: Responsiveness/SLAs 1, Quality/CSAT 2, Knowledge 2, Escalation/feedback 1, Retention/health 1, Team 2 (early burnout signs).

**60 days:** Drafted first SLA policy (P1–P4 with response/resolution targets, business-hours coverage honestly stated); negotiated an OLA with Eng to ack escalated P1s in 30 min and a weekly escalation sync; top-3 = (1) publish SLAs + burn down the backlog, (2) build the feedback loop so recurring bugs reach Product with evidence, (3) stand up a basic health score to catch at-risk accounts. Priya signed off; secured one additional agent req.

**90 days:** Published SLAs and ran a focused backlog burn-down (180 → 22, oldest now < 3 days); shipped top-8 KB articles (deflection now measurable at ~12%); ran the first evidenced feedback case to Product — "payment-timeout" theme = 412 tickets/quarter, cited in 2 of 3 churns — and it's now on the roadmap; stood up a Green/Yellow/Red health score and caught 2 at-risk enterprise accounts, both saved. Metrics live: CSAT, SLA attainment, deflection, gross churn, NRR.

**Need:** air cover to push back on Sales over-promising go-live dates, and Priya's backing on the Eng escalation OLA.
