# Triple Bottom Line — Analogy Bridge
# បាតបន្ទាត់ទាំងបី — ស្ពានប្រៀបធៀប

*Author: ichamrong | Date: 2026-05-31*

---

## Analogy 1: The Three-Legged Cooking Stove / ចង្ក្រានបីជើង

**The Story:**
The traditional Cambodian clay stove (ចង្ក្រាន) stands on three legs. Put the pot on a stove with one strong leg and two stubs, and the pot tips and the soup spills — no matter how strong that one leg is. The stove only holds the pot when all three legs reach the ground evenly.

A business is the same. Profit is one leg. People is the second. Planet is the third. A company can have an enormously strong Profit leg, but if the People leg and the Planet leg are stubs, the whole thing tips over — through strikes, scandals, lawsuits, or a poisoned river the community will no longer tolerate.

**The Mapping:**

| Analogy Element | Triple Bottom Line Element | ខ្មែរ |
|---|---|---|
| Leg one | Profit / economic result | ប្រាក់ចំណេញ |
| Leg two | People / social result | មនុស្ស |
| Leg three | Planet / environmental result | ភពផែនដី |
| The pot of soup | Long-run viability of the firm | ភាពរស់រានរយៈពេលវែង |
| A pot that tips | Business collapse from neglected legs | ការដួលរលំ |

**Where the Analogy Breaks Down:**
A stove's three legs never conflict — they all simply point down. A firm's three lines often *do* conflict: spending on the Planet leg can shrink the Profit leg in the short run. Balancing them is a live trade-off, not a fixed casting.

## Analogy 2: The Family Report Card / បណ្ណពិន្ទុគ្រួសារ

**The Story:**
Imagine a student who brings home a report card with a single grade: "Money earned from tutoring younger kids — A+." The parents are pleased. Then they discover the student skipped every class to do the tutoring (failing grade in school) and was rude to the neighbors who hired them (failing grade in conduct). One A+ hid two F's.

A wise parent does not look at one grade. They look at the whole card: academics, conduct, and earnings together. Only then do they know how the child is really doing.

**The Mapping:**

| Analogy Element | Triple Bottom Line Element | ខ្មែរ |
|---|---|---|
| Earnings grade | Profit | ប្រាក់ចំណេញ |
| Conduct grade | People | មនុស្ស |
| Academic/long-term grade | Planet (the future foundation) | ភពផែនដី |
| A single grade shown | Single-line financial accounting | គណនេយ្យបន្ទាត់តែមួយ |
| The full report card | Triple Bottom Line reporting | របាយការណ៍ TBL |

**Where the Analogy Breaks Down:**
A report card is graded by a neutral teacher. In business, companies often grade their *own* People and Planet lines — which is exactly how Elkington's idea got turned into "self-given A's," the abuse he later tried to recall.

## Analogy 3: The Fish Pond / ស្រះត្រី

**The Story:**
A family keeps a fish pond. Each year they can scoop out fish to sell — that is their profit. A greedy owner scoops out every fish, including the breeding stock, and has a spectacular year. The next year the pond is empty. A wise owner takes only what the pond can regrow, keeps the water clean (Planet), and shares the harvest fairly with the relatives who help tend it (People). Their yearly catch is smaller — but it continues forever.

**The Mapping:**

| Analogy Element | Triple Bottom Line Element | ខ្មែរ |
|---|---|---|
| Fish sold this year | Profit | ប្រាក់ចំណេញ |
| Breeding stock & clean water | Planet / natural capital | ដើមទុនធម្មជាតិ |
| The relatives who tend the pond | People / social capital | ដើមទុនសង្គម |
| Emptying the pond for one big year | Profit-only "good year" that destroys capital | ឆ្នាំល្អក្លែងក្លាយ |
| A pond that yields forever | Sustainable, regenerative business | អាជីវកម្មនិរន្តរ |

**Where the Analogy Breaks Down:**
A pond has clear natural limits the owner can see. A firm's social and environmental limits are often invisible until crossed — which is why we *measure* the three lines rather than relying on intuition.

---

## Core Insight Across All Three Analogies / ការយល់ដឹងស្នូល

All three show the same lesson: **a single measure of success invites you to destroy what it does not count.** The stove needs three legs, the report card needs three grades, the pond needs its stock and its keepers. Profit alone is the strong leg, the A+, the big catch — and on its own, it tips the pot, hides the F's, and empties the pond.
